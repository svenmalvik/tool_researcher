# Community Research Framework

Use this framework to capture public practitioner sentiment without over-weighting anecdotes.

## Goal

Document what public users and practitioners are saying about the candidate tool and the most relevant alternatives in a way that helps the decision.

## Preferred Source Mix

Use a mix of source types where relevant:

- GitHub issues, discussions, and release threads
- Reddit and Hacker News
- Stack Overflow and vendor community forums
- Review sites such as G2 or Capterra
- Independent blog posts, migration notes, and postmortems
- Social posts from identifiable practitioners when they contain concrete experience

## Evidence Labels

Label material findings using these categories:

- Verified fact: can be confirmed from official docs, source records, or direct product evidence
- Community report: a user or practitioner claim that is not independently verified
- Inference: a conclusion drawn from multiple facts or reports

## Per-Tool Questions

For each tool, answer questions such as:

- What do users consistently praise?
- What complaints recur across multiple independent sources?
- What seems to break adoption, rollout, or daily use?
- What do users say about support quality, pricing friction, or procurement?
- Are there recurring reliability, performance, lock-in, or governance concerns?
- Are the main complaints still current or mostly historical?
- Does the community discussion align with vendor positioning?

## Quality Checks

- Note recency so old complaints do not get treated as current reality by default.
- Note whether complaints cluster around a specific plan, platform, time period, or user segment.
- Distinguish beginner friction from expert critique where possible.
- Do not infer market share or product quality from thread volume alone.
- Do not let launch hype, social pile-ons, or likely astroturfing dominate the conclusion.

## Suggested Output Shape

For each tool, summarize:

- Signal strength
- Recurring positives
- Recurring negatives
- Adoption or implementation friction
- Caveats and bias
- Decision impact

Then compare the tools on:

- Signal quality and source diversity
- Most credible praise
- Most credible complaints
- Risks that deserve validation before a decision
