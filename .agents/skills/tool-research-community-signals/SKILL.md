# Tool Research Community Signals

## Overview

Use this skill when public community evidence should inform the evaluation of a tool. This work complements product facts, vendor documentation, and baseline analysis. It helps surface practitioner-reported strengths, weaknesses, rollout friction, support quality, and trust signals that may not be obvious from official sources.

## Workflow

1. Confirm which tool or tools are in scope and why community evidence matters to the decision.
2. Read `references/community-research-framework.md`.
3. Collect public community evidence for one tool at a time from multiple source types where available.
4. Normalize the evidence into recurring themes such as praise, complaints, adoption friction, support experience, reliability or performance concerns, switching pain, and gaps between vendor messaging and reported experience.
5. Label each material point as verified fact, community report, or inference.
6. Note source diversity, recency, and likely bias so isolated hot takes do not dominate the writeup.
7. Produce per-tool community notes and a short cross-tool comparison of the most credible signals.
8. Hand off the findings to `tool-research-tool-profile` for per-tool pages and to `tool-research-analysis` for synthesis.

## Required Output

Produce:

- Community signal summary for each tool in scope
- Recurring positive themes
- Recurring negative themes
- Adoption and implementation friction notes
- Support, reliability, or trust signals when visible
- Evidence caveats such as sparse, outdated, skewed, or vendor-amplified discussion
- Implications for fit, risk, and confidence

## Working Rules

- Prefer multiple independent sources over one loud thread.
- Do not present sentiment as representative when the evidence is thin.
- Do not confuse review-site marketing copy with user testimony.
- Separate community-reported experience from verified product behavior.
- Give more weight to recent, concrete, detailed reports than vague praise or dunk posts.
- Community evidence should sharpen the recommendation, not replace baseline and tool-profile work.

## Handoff

Pass the resulting notes into `tool-research-tool-profile` and `tool-research-analysis`.
